I am passionate about design and bringing stakeholders into the design process. When I first heard about Autodesk's generative design software in Fusion360, I was excited about the potential of codesigning with my laptop and Autodesk's technology. Generative design should reduce barriers to entry to design and engineering and eventually render mastery obselete.

My exploration of Autodesk's generative design software shaped my Bachelor's of Design thesis topic on the accessibility of design through intuitive inputs and generative outputs. Above are some of my explorations of generative design in Fusion360 and below are maps of what I think generative design means for the design industry.

Purpose:
Explore the scope of Autodesk's generative design software, theorize on the impact of the technology once it is more widely available.

Process:
Design products with Fusion360 generative design and note where it can be improved, refined or rendered more accessible.

Payoff:
Deep insight into the future of design through generative software. I will update this page with work from my thesis project as it develops.

Payoff:
Generative design will render the implementation phase of design obselete.

Fusion360 Generative Design software is a signal of what's to come. Designers, engineers and everyday users alike will embrace the human role of visionary while software produces products and more software for us. Implementation will be automated and human creativity will be augmented.

Vision and a deep understanding of users will usurp hard skills like 3D modelling, prototyping and drawing.
